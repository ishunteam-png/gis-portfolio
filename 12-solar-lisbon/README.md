# Project 12 — Lisbon Rooftop Solar Potential

**Per-building annual photovoltaic generation potential across 450 buildings in 5 Lisbon neighbourhoods. Combines OSM building polygons, Copernicus 25 m DEM (for roof slope + aspect inference and skyline shading), and PVGIS-anchored pvlib irradiance. Total: 12.5 GWh/year — enough to power 4,178 Portuguese households.**

## Why Lisbon

- **38.7°N** — prime mid-latitude solar profile, sun never north of zenith
- **2,800–3,100 GHI hours/year** — top quartile in Europe (Madrid: 2,750, Berlin: 1,600)
- **South-facing Atlantic coast** — no inland morning shadow
- **Policy tailwind** — Portuguese 2024 rooftop-PV credit (~30% income-tax offset) + 15-year net metering
- **Mixed building stock** — Pombaline grid + medieval Alfama + 20C blocks + seaside — five very different typologies in one AOI

## Pipeline

```
OSMnx → building polygons + roof:slope / roof:direction tags (where present)
      ↓
Copernicus 25 m DEM → centroid elevation + 9-cell aspect
      ↓
Per building: roof tilt + azimuth inference
   1. OSM roof:slope / roof:direction (accurate but rare)
   2. Neighbourhood-typical prior (Pombaline ≈ flat, Alfama ≈ 25° tile)
   3. DEM-derived terrain aspect (weak fallback)
      ↓
Skyline ray-cast (16 rays × 200 m) → obstruction factor 0..1
      ↓
PVGIS / pvlib annual irradiance for (lat, tilt, azimuth)
      ↓
annual_kwh = irradiance × usable_area × panel_eff × performance_ratio × (1 − obstruction)
      ↓
Dashboard JSON: per-building kWh, ranked
```

Constants:
- `panel_eff` = 0.20 (mono-Si module)
- `performance_ratio` = 0.83 (inverter + temperature + soiling + losses)
- `usable_roof_pct` = 0.75 (no-build margin for parapets, vents, walkways)
- `LISBON_GHI_FLAT` = 1,820 kWh/m²/yr (PVGIS Lisbon centroid)

Single pipeline: [`scripts/solar.py`](scripts/solar.py).

## What broke (worth knowing)

1. **OSM roof tags are sparse.** Only ~12% of Lisbon buildings have `roof:slope` or `roof:shape`; the rest need a typology fallback. Initial run treated all roofs as flat — over-estimated production in Alfama by ~25%. Switched to per-neighbourhood priors anchored on Lisbon Municipal Council 2023 roof typology survey.
2. **Skyline shading is not skyline → flat horizon.** A 25-m apartment block 30 m away casts a horizon angle of ~40° at the receiving roof — that obscures the sun for ~3 hours every winter day. Raw flat-horizon model under-counted obstruction by ~15% in narrow medieval streets. Switched to 16-ray cast against the neighbouring DEM cells.
3. **Direct + diffuse + reflected matters.** A south-facing 30° tilt gets ~110% of flat-horizon GHI from direct + diffuse. But east-facing roofs still get 88% (mostly diffuse). Cheap approximations that only model direct miss the diffuse contribution and over-penalise non-optimal orientations. Use the full Pérez transposition model in `pvlib`.
4. **Performance ratio drifts.** 0.83 is a good 2024 default, but actual measured PR for new Portuguese installs is 0.78–0.81 (hot roofs reduce module efficiency by ~5%/season summer). For a real customer proposal, fit PR per building from a 2-week installation telemetry period.

## Limitations and what I'd build next

1. **3D building heights.** Lisbon LOD-1 building heights are in Câmara Municipal de Lisboa's open dataset. Adding building height = better shadow modelling (cast not just received) + lets us drop tall-building shadows onto shorter neighbours.
2. **Hourly time-series PV simulation.** Annual kWh is the headline; for an installer the per-hour summer-noon vs winter-morning split matters for sizing the inverter. Hourly `pvlib.modelchain.ModelChain` swap is one extra dimension.
3. **Self-consumption + battery sizing.** A 200 m² south roof can generate more than the building consumes; how much battery + grid export does that imply? Pair with INE per-household consumption profile.
4. **Pre-1755-earthquake buildings.** Lisbon's medieval Alfama survived but most pre-quake construction is now structurally fragile. Some buildings physically can't support 200 kg of panels. Add a structural-feasibility flag from CMLisboa's Heritage Building register.
5. **Multi-city benchmarking.** Same pipeline on Madrid, Athens, Marseille, Naples for a mid-latitude European rooftop solar comparison.
