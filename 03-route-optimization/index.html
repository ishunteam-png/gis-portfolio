Three algorithms on the same 60-stop / 5-vehicle problem. Distance matrix is shortest-path travel time on the Tbilisi drive graph (2,694 nodes, 6,097 edges). Service time 180 s per stop, hard time windows.

OR-Tools ran 5 s with Guided Local Search. The greedy baseline is what most people would call "common-sense routing"; Clarke-Wright is the 1964 algorithm that's still competitive against modern solvers; OR-Tools wins on max-route time (the dimension that matters if drivers get paid by the hour or have their own time window).
